# AMENDED EXCLUSIVE LICENSEE & DISTRIBUTION AGREEMENT
## Multiple Natures International, LLC and Sussman Education

**Date**: April 24, 2026  
**Replaces**: Exclusive Licensee & Distribution Agreement, September 1, 2023  
**Status**: Draft for Legal Review & Discussion

---

## PREAMBLE

This amendment affirms and restructures the exclusive distribution rights granted to Sussman Education within the New York City K-12 education sector, contingent upon active performance against defined milestones.

The original agreement (Sept 1, 2023) granted exclusivity based on projected sales capacity. This amendment converts exclusivity into a **performance-based right** tied directly to measurable actions required to unlock the NYC Board of Education audit and FAMIS system approval—the commercial bottleneck preventing sales activation.

**Core Intent**: Exclusivity is not passive. It is earned and maintained through active participation in removing the DOE approval barrier.

---

## 1. UPDATED TERRITORY & EXCLUSIVITY

### 1.1 Territory Definition (Unchanged)
- **Territory**: New York City School District (K-12 sector)
- **Sector**: K-12 education (public schools)
- **Geographic Scope**: Five NYC Boroughs

### 1.2 Conditional Exclusivity (NEW)

Sussman Education holds **exclusive distribution rights** in the Territory and Sector, **conditional upon**:

1. Active participation in DOE/FAMIS approval pathway
2. Completion of performance milestones (Section 2, below)
3. Maintenance of minimum engagement standards (Section 3, below)

**If performance milestones are not met within defined timeframes**, exclusivity will:
- First tier (90 days): reduce to non-exclusive arrangement
- Second tier (120+ days): be revoked, opening Territory to additional partners

### 1.3 Non-Exclusive Territory Expansion (Unchanged)
Sussman may explore business opportunities **outside the Territory** where licensees do not exist, with written notice to MNI.

## 2. PERFORMANCE MILESTONES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

The following milestones must be completed within **90 calendar days** of agreement execution to maintain exclusivity.

### 2.1 Demand Creation Milestone

**Objective**: Generate documented school/district interest to create demand signals visible to NYC DOE.

**Requirement**: Secure written statements from **at least 3–5 NYC public schools or school districts** stating:

- They wish to purchase/implement the MNTEST tool
- They are awaiting NYC BOE approval/FAMIS listing to proceed
- Delay is materially impacting their ability to serve students

**Format**: Email, letter, or signed statement acceptable. Must include:
- School/District name
- Contact name & title (principal, administrator, or procurement officer)
- Explicit statement of purchase intent contingent on approval
- Date signed

**Deliverable**: Sussman provides MNI with copies of all demand letters by **Day 60** of this agreement.

**Rationale**: Schools requesting approval creates political and procedural pressure DOE cannot ignore.

### 2.2 Formal Pressure Letter Coordination

**Objective**: Coordinate schools to send formal request letters to NYC DOE Procurement/Vendor Onboarding.

**Requirement**: Sussman must:

1. Draft sample letter language (MNI can review/approve)
2. Deliver letters to identified school/district contacts
3. Ensure schools/districts send independently to DOE within **Day 75** of agreement
4. Provide MNI with copies of sent letters

**Sample Content** (Sussman to draft, MNI to approve):

> "[School Name] intends to purchase the MNTEST assessment tool from Sussman Education upon NYC Board of Education approval and FAMIS system listing. We request written timeline and clarity on any outstanding audit or approval requirements. This tool is critical to our student alignment and career pathway planning. Please advise on next steps and expected approval date."

**Deliverable**: Copies of school-submitted letters to DOE by **Day 75**.

### 2.3 Internal DOE Escalation Plan

**Objective**: Identify and engage the actual decision-maker(s) within DOE to move the audit forward.

**Requirement**: Within **Day 30**, Sussman must provide MNI with:

1. **Named Contact List**:
   - Specific individuals in DOE Procurement/Vendor Onboarding
   - FAMIS System Administration
   - Chief Technology Officer (or equivalent)
   - Any procurement officer with visibility on software audits

2. **Engagement Timeline**:
   - Initial contact: Day 30–40
   - Follow-up cadence: weekly or bi-weekly
   - Expected outcome/decision date from DOE

3. **Escalation Path** if no movement:
   - Who to escalate to (next level)
   - Timing for escalation
   - Coordination with MNI (if legal pressure becomes necessary)

**Deliverable**: Written escalation plan + contact list by **Day 30**.

### 2.4 Audit Status Documentation

**Requirement**: Sussman must obtain and deliver to MNI:

1. Current status of MNI software audit from DOE
2. Specific requirements or deficiencies (if any) blocking approval
3. Expected timeline for audit completion (in writing from DOE)

**If DOE claims no blockers but continues delaying**: This triggers legal escalation discussion (Section 4).

**Deliverable**: Documentation by **Day 45**.

## 3. MAINTENANCE STANDARDS

### 3.1 Monthly Reporting

Sussman must provide MNI with a brief monthly status report including:

- Contacts made to DOE/FAMIS team (names, dates, outcomes)
- School district feedback and progress
- Any barriers or new information about audit status
- Next 30-day priorities

### 3.2 Failure to Report

If Sussman fails to provide monthly reports for 2+ consecutive months, MNI may:
- Suspend exclusivity pending reinstatement of reporting
- Move to non-exclusive status if reporting is not restored within 30 days

---

## 4. SHARED LEGAL ESCALATION PATH

### 4.1 Shared Responsibility for DOE Pressure

If after 90–120 days the DOE audit remains incomplete **without documented blockers**, MNI will initiate legal escalation:

- **Article 78 petition** challenging unreasonable delay
- **FOIL requests** for internal DOE records on audit status
- **Political pressure** via schools, councils, education advocates

### 4.2 Sussman's Role in Escalation

Sussman agrees to:

1. **Support** legal proceedings (including providing testimony/documentation if needed)
2. **Authorize MNI** to reference Sussman's contracted revenue in legal filings
3. **Participate** in political pressure (schools, DOE contacts, advocacy partners)
4. **Share costs** of legal action (if escalation is necessary) on a **50/50 basis** for Article 78 and FOIL-related expenses

**Alternatively**: Sussman may fully fund legal costs, in which case MNI shares all outcomes and recovered damages/approval.

### 4.3 Legal Standing

MNI will position the claim as:

- Arbitrary and capricious delay by NYC BOE
- Breach of vendor approval process timelines (if such timelines exist in law)
- Interference with lawful commercial activity
- Potential damages: projected lost revenue ($125K year 1, $250K year 2, $350K year 3)

---

## 5. CONSEQUENCES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE

### 5.1 Milestone Non-Compliance (90 days)

If Sussman fails to meet **any** of the four milestones by **Day 90**:

- MNI may convert agreement to **non-exclusive** status immediately
- Sussman retains existing customer relationships and sales
- MNI may license to additional partners in NYC territory

### 5.2 Continued Non-Movement (120+ days)

If:

- DOE audit remains incomplete
- No documented deficiencies exist
- Sussman has not actively engaged on escalation

Then:

- Exclusivity **terminates**
- MNI may license to other distributors in NYC territory
- Sussman may continue existing sales but at **non-exclusive terms**
- Revenue sharing remains the same ($20/MNTEST, $750/Practitioner Course)

### 5.3 Failure to Support Legal Escalation

If escalation becomes necessary and Sussman refuses to:
- Provide documentation
- Allow use of contracted revenue figures
- Support political pressure

Then MNI may:
- Withdraw Sussman from exclusivity
- Pursue legal action independently
- Use all outcomes for benefit of MNI (not shared with Sussman)

# Sussman Meeting — April 30 Talking Points

## The frame to open with
*"We've been patient, and so have you. The schools want this. The superintendent has said so in writing. The only thing standing between us and real revenue is an undefined process inside DOE. Let's agree today on exactly what we're going to do — together — to get this unstuck."*

---

## What to ask Sussman to do (in priority order)

**1. Call Superintendent Esperance this week — ask for a formal letter**
She's already on record saying District 79 schools are "anxious to purchase." What's needed now is that same sentiment on D79 letterhead, signed, stating purchase intent is contingent only on DOE approval. That single letter changes the legal and bureaucratic weight of this request. It converts us from a vendor seeking permission into a tool that schools are being blocked from using.

**2. Find 2–4 more schools willing to put it in writing**
Sussman has the relationships. A handful of demand letters from principals or department heads saying "we want this, we're waiting on the cloud review" creates a pattern that's hard for DOE to ignore — and hard to leave unresolved indefinitely.

**3. Get a clear answer on the CMT batch question**
In November 2025, Kay Robbins asked whether items had been submitted to CMT with a batch number. Ron said no — he thought prior cloud review approval was needed first. This may be a simple procedural step that nobody communicated clearly. Sussman should ask Kay directly this week: *"Do we need to submit a CMT batch independently of the cloud review? If yes, we'll do it immediately."* And get the answer in writing.

**4. Get the ERMA question resolved — it's been 5 months**
In November, Kay said she was waiting for the ERMA unit to determine whether a new ERMA is required or just a product review. That question has been sitting for 5+ months. Sussman needs to follow up and get a written answer: which path is it, what does it require, who owns it, and what's the timeline?

**5. Request a real meeting — not another email — with Daphne Franklin or Alfred Davis**
Email follow-ups to Domion and Kay have run their course. Daphne Franklin (Sr. Director, Purchasing) and Alfred Davis (Chief of Staff, Division of School Leadership) were added to the thread in November. They have the authority to move this. A 20-minute call with either of them, backed by school demand letters, is worth six months of email chains.

**6. Engage the Office of Multilingual Learners**
They were copied on the November thread. ESL students in District 79 are the primary use case. Sussman should ask them directly: can your office formally endorse or support the ERMA request? Can you file an internal request to expedite? Their voice is an inside lever that Sussman and MNI can't pull from the outside.

## One thing to name clearly but without drama
The exclusive distribution agreement has run well past two deadlines — the original February 2025 and the extended March 2026. The spirit of the meeting isn't to be punitive about that; it's to acknowledge it and agree on a new set of concrete milestones that protect both sides. **The ask is not for more time — it's for a plan with specific actions and dates.**

---

## Framing to keep throughout
Not: *"you're behind, we have leverage."*
Instead: *"schools are ready, the DOE process is the only barrier, here's what we do together to break it loose."* The legal escalation path exists, but it doesn't need to be on the table for April 30. The goal of this meeting is a shared action plan, signed off by both sides, with named owners and real deadlines.

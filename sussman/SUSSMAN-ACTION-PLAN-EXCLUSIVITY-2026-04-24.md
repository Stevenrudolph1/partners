## PART 1: EXPEDITING THE NYC DOE CLOUD AUDIT

### 1.1 The Core Lever — School Demand Letters

The single most effective action Sussman can take is generating **formal, written purchase intent from schools**.

When a school or superintendent formally states "we intend to purchase this tool pending DOE approval," it:
- Converts MNI from "vendor seeking approval" to "vendor schools are waiting on"
- Creates internal DOE pressure to process the request
- Establishes documented, quantified commercial harm if DOE continues to delay
- Provides legal standing for escalation if necessary

**What Sussman must do:**

Obtain written statements from **at least 3–5 NYC public schools or districts** that include:

- School/district name and address
- Name and title of signatory (principal, assistant principal, department head, superintendent)
- Statement that the school intends to purchase MNTEST or MNI books
- Statement that purchase is contingent only on DOE/FAMIS approval
- Statement that delay is impacting the school's ability to serve students

**District 79 (Superintendent Esperance) is the natural first call.** She has already stated in writing that her schools are "anxious to purchase" and that students need the tool. A formal purchase intent letter from her office, on letterhead, is the highest-value action item on this list.

**Target**: Letters from D79 + 2–4 additional schools within **30 days**.

## PART 3: THE LEGAL ESCALATION PATH

MNI is prepared to escalate legally if DOE does not act. Sussman's cooperation is required.

### 3.1 What Legal Escalation Looks Like

If the action plan above does not produce an audit initiation within 90 days, MNI's legal team will:

1. **Send a formal demand letter** from counsel to DOE Purchasing, Legal, and School Leadership
2. **File a FOIL request** for all internal DOE records on the cloud review request
3. **Pursue an Article 78 proceeding** if necessary — compelling DOE to complete the review or explain the basis for delay

### 3.2 What We Need from Sussman for Legal Escalation

- **Purchase intent letters from schools** — these are the foundation of any damages claim
- **Authorization to reference Sussman's contracted revenue projections** in legal filings ($125K Year 1, $250K Year 2, $350K Year 3)
- **Participation in political escalation** — emails or calls to DOE contacts from Sussman's own network
- **Support for, not resistance to, formal legal demand** — Sussman should be a partner in this process, not a bystander

### 3.3 The Framing for Schools and DOE

When Sussman communicates to schools and DOE about expediting, the right framing is:

> "District 79 and other schools have told us they want this tool. The only barrier is the cloud review process. We are committed to making this available before the next school year. We need your help to move this forward."

Not: "our vendor is pressuring us." Not: "we are being threatened." Just: "schools want this, we want to deliver it, help us get it done."

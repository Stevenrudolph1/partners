## Part One — Summary

### The relationship

Since 2012, SOS Children's Villages India has partnered with Multiple Natures International (MNI) to bring the MNTEST diagnostic to children under its care. Fourteen years. Thousands of children assessed. Dozens of workshops conducted across India for village directors, coworkers, and senior staff. A continuous, trust-built collaboration that has quietly shaped how SOS thinks about each child's unique nature and path.

In 2026, that partnership is ready to expand.

### The moment

SOS today operates in a transformed landscape. Funders, donors, and institutional partners increasingly demand demonstrated, measurable impact. Every program is asked to prove — not assert — its value to the children it serves and the society it supports. At the same time, new tools, new data, and new methods make that proof achievable at a scale and depth that was impossible a decade ago.

Alongside this, two persistent operational realities: coordinators spend days of research per child on college, scholarship, and eligibility decisions — time they cannot spend on caregiving. And commercial placement agencies, the usual alternative, charge heavily and run on commissions from institutions — a structural conflict that prioritizes placement volume over child fit. SOS children deserve better on both counts.

This is an opportunity for SOS to lead: to show that the children in its care are not just sheltered, but *known*, *prepared*, and *launched* into lives of dignity and contribution — with evidence that bears scrutiny.

### What we are proposing

A next-generation program built on top of the MNTEST foundation, comprising:

- **Xavigate Map (SOS Edition)** — a personalized diagnostic report for each child. Coordinator enters basic intake; the system does the research, eligibility matching, and shortlisting. Days of work per child collapse into minutes. Fit-driven — MNI takes no commissions from institutions. Draws on a database of Indian higher-education institutions, programs, and scholarship eligibility, tailored to SOS context, incorporating SOS's own 14 years of ground-truth data.
- **Training course** — self-paced, available to all 50+ coworkers and directors, covering MN interpretation and Xavigate Map facilitation
- **Interpretation guides** — PDF and PowerPoint resources for coordinators working with children through their results
- **Webinars** — 8 per year across two tracks (coworker-facing + children-facing), delivered live by Steven Rudolph
- **SOS college data integration** — SOS's accumulated knowledge incorporated into the database as an SOS-verified layer
- **Research partnership** — a longitudinal study on MN-based guidance effectiveness, producing publishable evidence of SOS program outcomes
- **Coordinator support channel** — direct access to MNI for implementation questions

All of this is **additional to the existing MNTEST relationship**, which continues at its preferred partner rate.

### Investment

At retail, delivered line by line at 800 students per year, this program values at approximately **$139,050 in Year 1** and **$124,050 annually** thereafter.

The **SOS Partner Site License** consolidates everything into a single annual fee:

- **$20,000 per year** — unlimited students, unlimited coworkers, all components included
- **$7,500 one-time custom build fee** in Year 1 (reduced from a retail rate of $15,000) — covers SOS-specific intake form, SOS-branded report template, and SOS college data integration

**Year 1 total: $27,500. Year 2+: $20,000/year.** Plus MNTEST at its preferred partner rate.

### Why now

- The demand on SOS to demonstrate impact is real and growing. A rigorous, longitudinal study built on this platform will produce the kind of evidence that secures funding and recognition for decades.
- The MN-to-career mapping reflects India's current labor market, including new-economy roles, so recommendations stay relevant for the children entering college today.
- Scholarship coverage includes the schemes most relevant to SOS children — PM CARES for children affected by COVID orphanhood, central post-matric schemes for SC/ST/OBC/Minority students, and state-specific orphan policies.

### Next step

Review at your convenience. We're happy to walk through any component, adjust the shape, or answer questions. We'll move forward from there at a pace that works for SOS.

## Part Two — Detailed Proposal

### 1. About SOS Children's Villages India

SOS Children's Villages is the world's largest organization caring for children who have lost parental care. SOS India has operated villages across the country for over five decades, providing a family-like environment, education, healthcare, and a pathway to independent adulthood for thousands of children.

What distinguishes SOS is its long-term commitment: children are not processed through a system but raised — with a mother, siblings, a home, and a future. The operational reality of delivering on that promise, village by village, child by child, requires tools that match the seriousness of the mission.

### 2. About Multiple Natures International

Multiple Natures International is the publisher and steward of the Multiple Natures (MN) framework and the MNTEST diagnostic — a modification of Howard Gardner's Multiple Intelligences theory that extends the model to include nine Natures alongside ten Intelligences. MN provides a rigorous, non-reductive way to understand human difference without collapsing people into traits, types, or rankings.

Founder Steven Rudolph has worked directly with children, students, educators, parents, and institutions across India and internationally for over two decades to apply MN in practical, high-stakes contexts — including, since 2012, with SOS Children's Villages India.

### 3. Our Partnership Since 2012

For fourteen years, MNI and SOS have collaborated closely:

- **MNTEST administered to thousands of children across SOS villages** — providing a structured, evidence-based way to understand each child's unique profile of Natures and Intelligences
- **Dozens of workshops** conducted across India — for village directors, senior staff, coworkers, and in some cases children themselves — training on the framework, its interpretation, and its application in daily caregiving and educational decisions
- **Direct engagement from Steven Rudolph personally** — not delegated, not outsourced, with SOS leadership at the principal level
- **Preferred partner pricing** — MNTEST provided at a deeply discounted partner rate throughout the relationship, reflecting our long-standing commitment to SOS and the children it serves

### 4. The Present Moment — Challenges and Opportunities

SOS today operates in a transformed environment:

**Accountability pressure is rising.** Donors, corporate CSR partners, and institutional funders increasingly demand measurable impact. It is no longer sufficient to describe what a program does; one must prove what it produces. Child welfare organizations face particular scrutiny given the vulnerability of the populations they serve and the long time horizons over which outcomes emerge.

**Alumni outcome tracking is a gap.** SOS has produced generations of alumni, but systematic, longitudinal outcome data — the kind that would allow rigorous statements about program effectiveness — has been difficult to capture in a way that meets modern standards.

**Individual-child decision support is stretched.** Coordinators and directors carry the weight of consequential decisions for each child — what to study, where, when, how to finance it, which doors to knock on. These decisions compound across a child's life. Better tools reduce error and increase confidence.

**The research burden on coworkers is enormous.** Every child approaching college-age decisions generates hours of work for a coordinator: researching institutions, cross-referencing accreditations and rankings, checking hostel availability and orphan-quota policies, investigating scholarship eligibility scheme by scheme, calculating funding gaps, comparing placement records, verifying admission requirements. Multiplied across villages, across states, across thousands of children — this is time that cannot be spent on the actual work of raising children.

**Commercial placement agencies are not the answer.** The existing market for career and college placement in India is dominated by agencies that charge substantial fees and operate on commissions from institutions. That commission structure creates a built-in conflict of interest: the agency's incentive is to steer students toward institutions that pay the highest referral fees, not toward the institutions that best match the student's profile. For SOS children — whose futures depend on fit, not on placement volume — this model is actively harmful.

**The opportunity is now.** With the right infrastructure, SOS can convert fourteen years of MNTEST data, decades of operational knowledge, and its rich network of coworker observation into:
- Individualized, evidence-based guidance for every child
- Measurable outcomes that demonstrate program impact to funders
- A published body of research that positions SOS as a leader in rigorous, non-reductive child development
- A competitive edge that distinguishes SOS from less-equipped peers

This proposal is designed to meet exactly that opportunity.

### 5. What's Included in 2026

The current Xavigate Map includes several components that expand on earlier iterations of the partnership.

#### 5.1 Careers, tasks, and activities database

Children are matched against a taxonomy of **over 3,000 careers, tasks, and leisure activities** scored across 19 MN×MI dimensions. This replaces a narrow set of familiar career categories with a broader reference set that includes:

- Traditional professions (medicine, law, engineering, teaching, administration)
- Trade and vocational paths
- New-economy careers (data science, cybersecurity, AI/ML, digital design, content creation)
- Hybrid and emerging roles
- Tasks and functional work units — the components of a job, not just the job title
- Leisure and extracurricular activities — surfacing authentic interests that predict engagement beyond formal careers

Each item is scored on all 9 Natures and all 10 Intelligences, plus contextual dimensions, producing a signature that the Xavigate Map can match against a child's MN profile.

#### 5.2 Access to a higher-education database

The Xavigate Map draws on a structured database of Indian higher education. Current contents:

| | |
|---|---|
| Institutions | 91,375 |
| Programs | 245,186 |
| NAAC accreditations | 8,426 |
| NIRF rankings | 11,382 |
| Placement records | 12,041 |

For each institution, the system has access to:

- **Institution profile** — name, location (state and district), institutional type (government, private, deemed, central / state university, autonomous college, standalone institute), women-only status, founding year, website
- **Programs** — every program offered at each institution, normalized into a consistent schema: name, level (Diploma, UG, PG, PhD, Integrated), domain (engineering, medical, law, education, management, and others), duration, intake capacity, affiliating university, and approving body (AICTE, UGC, BCI, NMC, NCTE, state councils)
- **Accreditations** — NAAC grades and cycles, NBA program-level accreditations where applicable, validity windows
- **Rankings** — NIRF rankings by category and year, with scores
- **Placements** — where reported: placement rate, average and median salary, top recruiters, industry distribution, confidence tier (officially reported vs. derived from institutional disclosures)
- **Affiliations** — university relationships and autonomous status

Coverage includes:

- Engineering, technology, and polytechnic institutions
- Management, commerce, and business schools
- Medical, dental, pharmacy, nursing, paramedical, and AYUSH (Ayurveda / Homeopathy / Unani / Siddha / Naturopathy / Yoga) institutions
- Law colleges
- Teacher education colleges
- Design, architecture, and fine arts institutions
- General arts, science, humanities, and social science programs
- Open universities and distance-learning programs

What this means in practice: a coordinator working with an SOS child can see a current picture of an institution's accreditation status, recent placement performance, program offerings, intake capacity, and eligibility requirements — without calling the college, cross-referencing brochures, or relying on marketing material.

#### 5.3 SOS's own data — incorporated

SOS has spent fourteen years learning which colleges welcome its children, which hostels accept orphan-quota, which coordinators have established relationships with particular admissions offices, which institutions have delivered good outcomes for SOS alumni and which have not.

This ground-truth knowledge is an invaluable asset that generic sources cannot replicate. Under this proposal, SOS's accumulated data is incorporated into the Xavigate Map system as an **SOS-verified layer**:

- Tagged as SOS-authored
- Surfaced prominently in the matcher
- Weighted higher than general sources in recommendations
- Maintained as an ongoing service (SOS adds to it, we integrate it)

The result: the Xavigate Map doesn't just tell a child "here are technically eligible colleges" — it tells them "here are colleges that have actually worked for SOS children like you, with these specific institutions pre-vetted by coordinators who came before."

#### 5.4 Scholarship coverage

Access to eligibility rules for the scholarship schemes that matter most for SOS children:

- **PM CARES for Children** — for children orphaned by COVID-19
- **Central Sector Scheme** for merit-cum-means students
- **Post-Matric Scholarships** for SC, ST, OBC, and Minority students
- **Pradhan Mantri Scholarship Scheme (PMSS)** — for wards of ex-servicemen
- **Pragati Scholarship** — for girl students in technical education
- **Saksham Scholarship** — for students with disabilities
- **State-level schemes** — Maharashtra (orphan reservation), Tamil Nadu (post-matric), Rajasthan (Palanhar + MUSY), Delhi (SC/ST/OBC merit), Uttar Pradesh (Samaj Kalyan), Karnataka (SSP) — with more to come
- **Top-Class Education for SC Students** — for Institutes of National Importance

For any given child and any given institution, the Xavigate Map identifies which of these schemes the child is eligible to apply for — moving from generic scholarship lists to a specific, per-college funding picture with the remaining gap calculated.

#### 5.5 Customizable intake — tailored for SOS reality

The Xavigate Map begins with a structured intake that captures everything relevant to producing a meaningful recommendation. For SOS children, that includes context no commercial career tool asks about:

- SOS village and region
- Care stage (still in care, transitioning, alumni)
- Documentation status (Aadhaar, orphan certificate, caste certificate, domicile, family income affidavit)
- Financial context (family support, PM CARES eligibility, expected SOS contribution)
- Academic history (marks, subjects, board)
- Hostel requirement and quota eligibility
- Career preferences expressed in the child's own words
- Complete MN and MI scores from MNTEST

The intake is a 7-tab wizard designed to be filled in by a coordinator with the child, takes roughly 20–30 minutes, and produces all the inputs the Map engine needs.

#### 5.6 The SOS-branded Xavigate Map report

The output is a 14-page personalized PDF — a document the child can keep, the coordinator can reference, and the director can discuss with donors. Structure:

1. **Intake summary** — a snapshot of the child's situation
2. **Student profile** — MN and MI charts with narrative interpretation
3. **Career shortlist** — ranked recommendations based on MN×MI signature and stated preferences
4. **Documentation status** — a working checklist of what's filed and what's missing
5. **Financial plan** — per-college: total fees × PM CARES contribution × SOS funding × remaining gap
6. **College recommendations** — full cards for each recommended institution: NIRF rank, NAAC grade, placement data, salary data, hostel availability, scholarships available, application deadlines
7. **Backup options** — second-tier recommendations
8. **Coordinator checklist** — action items for the coordinator to follow up on
9. **Attribution and methodology** — how the recommendations were produced, referenced against SOS-verified data

The report is designed to be useful three ways: as a decision tool in conversation with the child, as a record in the child's SOS file, and as evidence when speaking with funders.

### 6. Components of This Program

#### 6.1 Xavigate Map (SOS Edition)

The flagship deliverable. One personalized 14-page PDF report per student. Generated from the intake form described in §5.5, pulling from the database described in §5.2–§5.4, producing the output described in §5.6.

#### 6.2 Training course — self-paced

A 6-hour self-paced course available to all SOS coworkers — directors, village mothers, coordinators, senior staff. Covers:

- The Multiple Natures framework — what it is, what it is not
- MNTEST administration and interpretation at depth
- Xavigate Map facilitation — how to sit with a child, walk through the report, make decisions from it
- Common failure modes — how to avoid reducing a child to a score, a typology, or a top-three career list
- Case studies — anonymized examples from SOS history and from MNI's practice

Access is unlimited — every current and future coworker can take the course at any time.

#### 6.3 Interpretation guides

Companion documents: PDF + PowerPoint. Designed for coordinators to use while working with a child on their results. Includes example language, common questions, how to handle resistance or confusion, how to connect MN results to the child's actual situation and options.

Updated annually with new examples and refined language.

#### 6.4 Webinars — 8 per year

Live sessions delivered by Steven Rudolph personally:

- **4 coworker-facing webinars** — training, Q&A, advanced topics, case discussions
- **4 children-facing webinars** — age-appropriate sessions for children working through their own results, with SOS coordinators present as facilitators

Recorded, archived, available to rewatch.

#### 6.5 SOS college data integration

SOS's accumulated college knowledge is ingested, normalized to the database schema, and tagged as SOS-verified during setup. New observations from SOS coordinators continue to be incorporated on an ongoing basis, with quarterly integration cycles. Part of the Year 1 custom build.

#### 6.6 Research partnership — the longitudinal study

Under an agreed MOU (not included in this proposal — separate document), MNI and SOS jointly design and execute a longitudinal study on the effectiveness of MN-based guidance for children in alternative care. Elements:

- Anonymized outcome tracking at 3, 5, and 10 years post-placement
- Cross-cohort comparisons — MN-guided vs. non-MN-guided where ethical and possible
- Qualitative case studies drawn from the coordinator and alumni network
- Joint publication in peer-reviewed and practitioner journals
- Co-branded white papers suitable for presentation to funders and institutional partners

MNI provides methodology, statistical analysis, coordination, and report authorship. SOS provides data access, alumni liaison, and narrative contribution.

The output is evidence — the kind SOS can put in front of any funder, any corporate partner, any journalist, and any government body that asks "what does your program actually accomplish?"

#### 6.7 Coordinator support channel

Direct access to MNI for implementation questions, edge cases, interpretation challenges, and data issues. Response within 48 hours during working days.

### 9. Why This Works

- **14 years of trust** — this is not a cold pitch. The decision is whether to deepen an already-working relationship.
- **Steven's personal involvement** — principal-level engagement, not delegated to a sales team or junior staff.
- **Hours returned to coordinators** — a coordinator fills out the intake form; the Xavigate Map does the research, eligibility checks, financial modeling, and shortlisting. What used to take days of investigation per child takes minutes. Time returns to what coordinators are actually there to do: raise children.
- **Fit-driven recommendations, not commission-driven** — MNI has no financial relationship with any institution in the database. Recommendations are produced by matching the child's profile to institutional fit, accreditation quality, placement outcomes, and verified SOS experience. No referral fees, no kickbacks, no incentive to steer.
- **Coverage matches SOS reality** — the database, the scholarship schemes, and the intake form are aligned to the specific situations SOS children face.
- **Research partnership produces publishable evidence** — this is how SOS demonstrates to the world what it already knows to be true.
- **Single site license** — no per-unit invoicing, no counting, no annual renegotiation friction.

## Executive Summary

For 14 years, SOS Children's Villages India has used MNTEST to understand how each child is wired — the Natures and Intelligences that make them who they are. With 700+ MNTESTs in the most recent tranche, the partnership has reached a scale where a deeper, more structured offering makes sense.

This proposal extends the MNTEST relationship into a complete **diagnostic + placement + training + research partnership** — purpose-built for SOS students and the coworkers who guide them.

Six components:

1. **Xavigate Map — SOS Edition.** A custom version of our flagship diagnostic, including a 25,000+ Indian college database and a 6-gate viability filter that accounts for the constraints SOS students actually face (documentation status, hostel availability, financial reality, orphan-specific support).
2. **Self-paced training course** for SOS coworkers on the MN framework and how to interpret MNTEST + Map results.
3. **Interpretation guides** (PDF + PowerPoint) for day-to-day coordinator use.
4. **Regular webinars** — one track for coworkers, one for children.
5. **Research partnership** — a longitudinal study of MN's effect on placement, career satisfaction, and earnings across SOS alumni cohorts.
6. **MNTEST continuity** — unchanged pricing, unchanged workflow.

## Why This Partnership Is Unique

The existing MNTEST is a strong foundation. What SOS and MNI have the opportunity to build is something no other diagnostic partnership in India looks like:

**The Xavigate Map is already customized for SOS realities.** We've built a college-matching system that filters 25,000+ Indian institutions against six viability gates — documentation, financial, hostel, placement, location, academic fit. These are the real constraints SOS students face that generic career tools ignore. The matcher surfaces colleges where a student can actually enroll, stay, graduate, and be placed — not just colleges they might theoretically qualify for.

**14 years of MNTEST data is a research goldmine.** No other institutional partner has this depth. If we collect follow-up outcomes — placement rate, career satisfaction, earnings trajectory — we can publish the first longitudinal study of Multiple Natures effectiveness with institutional-care youth. That study has value to SOS (advocacy, funding), to MNI (credibility, thought leadership), and to the broader child-welfare research community.

**The economics work because of the volume.** MNTEST at current rate, Xavigate Map at 80-90% discount, training at institutional bulk pricing — the unit costs are low enough that SOS can deploy the full stack across all students annually, not just special cases.

## What SOS Receives

### 1. Xavigate Map — SOS Edition

A bespoke version of the Xavigate Map diagnostic, customized for SOS:

**Custom intake form.** Captures what matters for SOS decisions: SOS village, care stage, documentation status (Aadhaar, caste certificate, domicile, orphan certificate), financial context (SOS funding amount, PM CARES eligibility), academic history, career interests, and Nature × Intelligence scores.

**Custom diagnostic report.** A 9-section PDF + HTML report designed for SOS coordinators:

- Intake summary — verbatim verification of what was entered
- Student profile — Natures, Intelligences, constraints, cluster fit
- Career shortlist — student's own picks routed to matching colleges, plus system shortlist
- Documentation status — what's in hand, what's missing, action items
- Financial plan — per-college fee × SOS funding × PM CARES × gap analysis
- Recommendations — full college cards with NIRF rank, NAAC grade, placement rate, average salary, why-this-fits
- Backup options — runner-up colleges if primary picks fail
- SOS Coordinator Checklist — numbered action items

**25,000+ college database.** The report is backed by a matcher that draws from AICTE, NIRF, NAAC, UGC, and aggregator scrapes — 36,000+ institutions with 126,000+ programs, ranking data, placement statistics, and fee information. As extraction completes, the database grows toward ~45,000 institutions.

**6-gate viability filter.** No college surfaces unless it passes: documentation (SOS student can get admitted), financial (SOS + scholarships cover it), hostel (residential option exists), placement (colleges that actually place students), location (reachable or relocation-viable), and academic fit (student meets admission requirements).

### 2. Self-Paced Training Course for Coworkers

A professionally-produced online course for SOS coworkers and coordinators:

- **Module 1:** The Multiple Natures framework — 9 Natures, their expression, and why each matters equally
- **Module 2:** Multiple Intelligences — the 10 intelligences and how they combine with Natures
- **Module 3:** Reading the MNTEST — scores, patterns, misinterpretations to avoid
- **Module 4:** Interpreting the Xavigate Map — student profile, constraints, recommendations
- **Module 5:** Practical application — career conversations with SOS students
- **Module 6:** Case studies — walk-throughs of real (anonymized) Map reports

Format: self-paced video + workbook + short quizzes. Estimated 6-8 hours total. Coordinators earn a completion certificate. Course is hosted on `xavigate-training`.

### 3. Interpretation Guides

For day-to-day use by coordinators who don't have time to revisit the full course:

- **Coordinator's Guide to MNTEST** — PDF + PPT, ~20 pages. How to read scores, common patterns, red flags.
- **Coordinator's Guide to the Xavigate Map** — PDF + PPT, ~30 pages. Section-by-section walkthrough of the SOS report with examples.
- **Quick-reference card** — laminated 2-page summary for daily use.

### 4. Regular Webinars

Two ongoing tracks:

**Coworker webinars (quarterly).** MNI team (Steven + team) host live sessions for SOS coordinators. Topics: interpreting complex profiles, handling specific student situations (low scores, unusual patterns, resistance), new research findings, Q&A. Recorded and archived for new hires.

**Children's webinars (quarterly).** Age-appropriate sessions for SOS youth on discovering their Natures, exploring careers, navigating the transition from care to independence. Interactive; includes workbook exercises.

### 5. Research Partnership

A longitudinal study, co-conducted by MNI and SOS India, examining:

- **Hypothesis:** Students whose MNTEST profile aligns with their chosen career path show better outcomes (retention, satisfaction, earnings) than those whose path diverges from their profile.
- **Cohort:** SOS students who have taken MNTEST from 2012 to present (potentially ~5,000+ individuals).
- **Variables:** Nature × Intelligence profile at assessment, career path taken, current occupation, satisfaction (short instrument), earnings band, retention in field.
- **Methodology:** Retrospective cohort analysis + forward-looking panel.
- **Output:** Peer-reviewed research paper, co-authored by MNI and SOS researchers.
- **Use:** Advocacy (SOS funding), credibility (MNI positioning), field contribution (child welfare research).

MNI contributes: assessment infrastructure (free/at-cost), analytical work, manuscript preparation.
SOS contributes: cohort access, follow-up data collection, IRB/ethics approvals, co-authorship.

### 6. MNTEST — Unchanged

MNTEST continues at current pricing and workflow. No changes required.

# Scope — Interpretation Guides for SOS Coordinators

**Status:** Not started. Scope defined; production required.
**Purpose:** Day-to-day reference for SOS coordinators after they've completed the training course.

---

## Why These Documents Exist

The training course (see [02-training-self-paced.md](02-training-self-paced.md)) teaches coordinators how to read MNTEST and Xavigate Map results. But coordinators won't rewatch videos every time a new student takes an assessment. They need:

- A document they can open on their phone or desk during a student meeting
- A quick-reference card pinned above their workstation
- A workshop deck when a new coordinator joins mid-year

These guides fill that gap.

---

## Deliverables

### 1. Coordinator's Guide to MNTEST
**Format:** PDF + PowerPoint
**Length:** ~20 pages PDF / 30 slides PPT
**Audience:** Coordinators interpreting MNTEST results without the full Map report

**Contents:**
- One-page overview: What MNTEST is and what it's not
- The 9 Natures — one page per Nature with expression examples
- The 10 Intelligences — one page per Intelligence
- How to read score ranges (don't fixate on top score)
- Common patterns:
  - Spiky profile (few high, most low)
  - Flat profile (similar mid-range scores)
  - High-congestion profile (several high scores)
  - Profile with extreme low scores
- What to do with each pattern (conversation direction)
- Red flags that warrant follow-up
- Common misinterpretations (and how to avoid)
- Sample conversation scripts (3)

### 2. Coordinator's Guide to the Xavigate Map (SOS Edition)
**Format:** PDF + PowerPoint
**Length:** ~30 pages PDF / 40 slides PPT
**Audience:** Coordinators working with full Map reports

**Contents:**
- One-page overview: The Map as a coordinator's tool
- Section-by-section walkthrough:
  - Section 0 — How to Read the report
  - Section 1 — Intake Summary (verify before proceeding)
  - Section 2 — Student Profile (Natures, Intelligences, constraints, cluster fit)
  - Section 3 — Career Shortlist (student's picks + system shortlist)
  - Section 4 — Documentation Status (what's actionable)
  - Section 5 — Financial Plan (reading the per-college math)
  - Section 6 — Recommendations (college cards — what each field means)
  - Section 7 — Backup Options
  - Section 8 — Coordinator Checklist
- The 6 viability gates explained in practical terms
- How to interpret "Limited public data" on a college
- What to do when the report produces sparse or no matches
- Handling student disappointment with recommendations
- Documentation action items — who to contact for what
- Financial plan — interpreting fee × scholarship × gap
- Sample coordinator-student conversation (full script)

### 3. Quick-Reference Card
**Format:** PDF (laminatable) + print-ready file
**Length:** 2 pages, double-sided
**Audience:** Coordinator's desk / wall

**Side 1 — MNTEST:**
- 9 Natures with one-line descriptors
- 10 Intelligences with one-line descriptors
- "Ask yourself" diagnostic questions

**Side 2 — Map:**
- 9 report sections
- 6 viability gates in one line each
- Key fields in a college card (NIRF, NAAC, placement, salary)
- "When to call MNI" checklist

## Design System Compliance

All documents follow the SR Design System:
- Libre Baskerville for titles, IBM Plex Sans for body
- Alignment/MN orange (#C96B3C) for accents
- Dusk Ember gradient for covers
- No emojis, no stock imagery
- Approved Lucide icons only (per `design-system/ICONS.md`)

Cover rendering follows [design-system/COVER-SYSTEM-V2.md](../../../design-system/COVER-SYSTEM-V2.md) — rendered as PNG/JPG images via `node render-cover.js`, never CSS-rendered.

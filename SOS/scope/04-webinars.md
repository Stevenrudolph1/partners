# Scope — Webinar Program for SOS

**Status:** Not started. Scope defined; delivery infrastructure partially in place.
**Two tracks:** Coworker webinars (coordinators, care staff) and Children's webinars (SOS youth).

---

## Why Webinars

Training course + interpretation guides handle the foundation. But things the static content can't do:

- Address new questions as coordinators encounter them in practice
- Let Steven (and team) share new thinking, research, or updates
- Give children a direct human connection to the framework, not just reports
- Build a learning community — coordinators learn from each other's questions, children see they're not alone in their uncertainty

Webinars are the live layer on top of the static content.

---

## Track 1 — Coworker Webinars

**Audience:** SOS coordinators, house parents, career counselors, regional leadership.
**Cadence:** Quarterly (4 per year).
**Duration:** 75 min each (60 content + 15 Q&A).
**Format:** Live on Zoom / Google Meet. Recorded. Archived for later viewing.

### Recurring topic themes (rotating)

- **MN in practice:** Deeper dive into one Nature or Intelligence, with examples from SOS-relevant contexts
- **Reading complex profiles:** Case walk-throughs with profiles that don't fit "easy" interpretations
- **Specific situations:** How to handle students who reject their Map, students with documentation gaps, students with very low scores, etc.
- **What's new:** Updates to the Map, new college data, new research findings, new tools
- **Community Q&A:** Open session where coordinators submit questions in advance and Steven / team answer

### Pre-webinar
- Topic announcement 2 weeks prior
- Coordinators submit questions via form
- Reading material (if any) sent 1 week prior

### Post-webinar
- Recording uploaded to xavigate-training archive
- Workbook / handout (1-2 pages) distributed
- Follow-up Q&A via email thread for 1 week after

## Track 2 — Children's Webinars

**Audience:** SOS youth ages 14-20. Small-group or cohort format preferred over mass webinar.
**Cadence:** Quarterly (4 per year).
**Duration:** 60 min each.
**Format:** Interactive — not a lecture. Live on Zoom with breakout rooms or group chat for exercises.

### Recurring topic themes (rotating)

- **Discovering your Natures:** Introduction for new students or refresher — interactive exercise
- **Career exploration:** How to explore options when you don't know what's out there yet
- **The transition:** Care → independence. What the Map says about your path. What to expect.
- **Real stories:** Alumni of SOS (age-appropriate) share their journeys — MN-informed but grounded

### Size and logistics
- Recommended: 15-30 students per session (breakout rooms feasible)
- Language: primarily English; regional language co-facilitators if needed (separate quote)
- Coordinator presence required (context for students, follow-up support)
- Age grouping: 14-16 and 17-20 may need separate sessions — some topics differ in maturity

### Pre-webinar
- Coordinator briefs students on topic a few days prior
- Simple prep exercise students complete before joining

### Post-webinar
- Recording NOT distributed publicly (privacy)
- Summary sent to coordinators only
- Follow-up reflection exercise for students to do with coordinator

## Delivery Infrastructure

### What exists
- Zoom / Google Meet accounts (MNI)
- Webinar landing page templates on xavigate.com
- Email announcement system (MailerLite `institutional` group for directors; separate list for coordinators)
- Recording archive capability on xavigate-training

### What needs setup
- Dedicated SOS webinar calendar (public to SOS only)
- Coordinator enrollment workflow (registers once, notified of all sessions)
- Children's webinar registration per-cohort (coordinators register their students)
- Post-webinar workbook distribution automation

## Dependencies

- SOS committed to partnership (signed agreement)
- SOS primary contact for coordinator list + scheduling
- Children's privacy / consent procedures agreed (no recording, no public sharing)
- Steven's calendar — 4 quarterly sessions (coworker) + 4 quarterly (children) = 8 sessions/year to block

---

## Risks

- **Low attendance early on.** Mitigate by initial coordinator-led push + making attendance part of coordinator expectations.
- **Children's webinar cultural fit.** Some topics may land differently in the Indian SOS context than Western examples. Mitigate by co-designing topics with SOS India team, not imposing curriculum.
- **Time zone:** India is 9.5-12.5 hours ahead of US Pacific. Need a schedule that works for both Steven and SOS coordinators. Likely Steven's morning = SOS evening.
- **Bandwidth:** Some SOS villages have limited internet. Mitigate by sending audio-only backup + transcripts.

## Out of Scope

- Ad-hoc emergency consultations (separate rate)
- Steven traveling to India in person (separate project)

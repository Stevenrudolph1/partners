# Scope — Self-Paced Training Course for SOS Coworkers

**Status:** Not started. Scope defined; production required.
**Target audience:** SOS coworkers, coordinators, house parents, career counselors.
**Format:** Self-paced video + workbook + quizzes on xavigate-training LMS.
**Estimated duration:** 6-8 hours completion time (not content length — includes exercises).

---

## Why This Course Exists

SOS coordinators receive MNTEST and Xavigate Map reports for students in their care. Without training, they interpret these reports through whatever intuition they have — which varies. This course gives every SOS coworker a common foundation:

- What MN is and isn't (not a personality test, not typology, not ranking)
- How to read an MNTEST score without misinterpreting it
- How to read a Xavigate Map report end-to-end
- How to have a productive career conversation with a student using the report
- When to flag something for follow-up (low scores, unusual patterns, student resistance)

The goal: after the course, a coordinator can sit with a student, open the Map report, and guide a useful 30-minute conversation without calling MNI.

## Module Structure

### Module 1 — The Multiple Natures Framework
**Duration:** ~60 min
- What "Nature" means in the MN framework (not archetype, not type)
- The 9 Natures: Administrative, Adventurous, Creative, Educative, Entertaining, Entrepreneurial, Healing, Protective, Providing
- Why each Nature is of equal value (critical — GOV-00 Principle 12)
- Common misinterpretations to avoid ("top" nature, "strongest" trait, ranking)
- Examples across professions

### Module 2 — Multiple Intelligences
**Duration:** ~60 min
- The 10 intelligences in the modified Howard Gardner framework
- Gross Bodily, Fine Bodily, Interpersonal, Logical, Linguistic, Graphic Visual, Spatial Visual, Musical, Intrapersonal, Naturalistic
- How MIs differ from IQ
- Why MIs also rank equally, not hierarchically
- Nature × MI interactions — how a Nature expresses through different MIs

### Module 3 — Reading the MNTEST
**Duration:** ~90 min
- What MNTEST measures and how
- The two score reports: Natures and Intelligences
- How to read the profile holistically (not cherry-picking top scores)
- Common patterns (flat profile, spiky profile, high-congestion profile)
- Red flags worth a follow-up conversation
- Case study walk-through (fictional profile — student 1)

### Module 4 — Interpreting the Xavigate Map (SOS Edition)
**Duration:** ~120 min
- Report section-by-section walkthrough
- Section 0: How to Read — orientation for the coordinator
- Section 1: Intake Summary — verification before proceeding
- Section 2: Student Profile — Natures, Intelligences, constraints, cluster fit bars
- Section 3: Career Shortlist — student's chosen careers + system shortlist
- Section 4: Documentation Status — action items
- Section 5: Financial Plan — per-college fee × PM CARES × SOS funding
- Section 6: Recommendations — full college cards
- Section 7: Backup Options
- Section 8: Coordinator Checklist
- The 6 viability gates explained
- How to handle "no matches" or sparse results
- Case study walk-through (fictional profile — student 2)

### Module 5 — Career Conversation with an SOS Student
**Duration:** ~90 min
- Setting: what the coordinator does with the report
- Pre-conversation prep (what to read first)
- Opening the conversation (what to ask, what to avoid)
- Walking the student through the student-facing sections
- Handling resistance, disappointment, or unexpected reactions
- Closing: documenting follow-ups
- Role-play scripts (3 scenarios)

### Module 6 — Three Case Studies
**Duration:** ~90 min
- Three anonymized fictional profiles (different ages, states, MN profiles, career paths)
- For each: full report walkthrough + hypothetical conversation outcome
- Emphasis on diversity of outcomes (not every student "should become" an engineer)
- Capstone exercise: coordinator designs a follow-up plan for one of the three

## Format and Delivery

### Hosting
Course lives on [xavigate-training](https://xavigate-training.pages.dev/) (existing Cloudflare Pages deployment). Steven's CLAUDE.md notes git push alone doesn't auto-deploy — runs `npx wrangler pages deploy` manually.

### Content components
- **Videos:** ~60 min total recorded presentation (Steven + team)
- **Slides:** PPT for each module, also available as PDF
- **Workbook:** PDF, ~40 pages, reflection prompts + exercises
- **Quizzes:** 5-10 questions per module, short-answer and scenario-based
- **Case studies:** 3 full fictional Map reports, downloadable as PDFs
- **Certificate of completion:** Generated when all modules + quizzes pass

### Student path
- Self-paced (no cohort requirement)
- Average completion: 6-8 hours over 1-2 weeks
- Coordinator keeps access for reference after completion

### Updates
- Quarterly content updates (minor)
- Annual major review
